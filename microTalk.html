Recording a microTalk podcast

This website will briefly go over everything you need to start the podcast

Click on a tab to open for info on anything you might need
You can also watch the video I made for reference

Setting up the Equipment

Taking everything out

This list will be pretty short since there isn't much to make sure of what you have on hand
A list of things that should be taken out are:

  • MixPre10
  • Microphones
  • Do not disturb sign

Everything needed to turn on the MixPre10 is in one bag

The microphones and cables for the microphones should be all in a clear bag

If you are recording for the podcast, make sure to have a release form printed out for the guest to sign. Otherwise, the podcast might not be allowed to be released.

MixPre10 setup

Since the microphones and cables are numbered, just be sure to connect them to the numbered ports so it's easier to troubleshoot in case something isn't going as planned.

The power on switch is numbered 15 on the image

You should also remember to connect the dongle to the headphone port and not the AUX port

On the image, it should be numbered 18

Configuring the MixPre10

Adjusting the knobs

After powering on the MixPre10 the indicator should be red and the screen should turn on

The indicator is numbered 1

After it's powered on, microphones are connected and turned on, you can start turning the knob to see if sound is getting picked up by the microphone.

When moving the numbered knobs, the screen should tell you what level they are at
the levels should be around 48 to 52. This varies on how loud or low the speaker is

If the speaker too much of a loud voice, it can go just a bit lower than 48. If a speaker speaks too low, you can set the knob to go to 52, and should remind them to be a little closer to the microphone so everyone can hear them better and to ensure that the microhpone picks up on their voice.

Note: It is not recommended to go over 52 since that's where i notice the microphone starts picking up background noise from the room.

Arming the Mics

A very important step is to make sure that the mics are ARMED

This is so a separate mono file can be recorded for each person. In case of a loud cough, too much background noise, or other issue comes from one mic, it can be isolated and fixed.

To check if a microphone is armed, you can tap on the screen and see if the numbers are red

If they are in gray, then you need to ARM them

To ARM, push in the knob of the microphone not currently ARMed

A new screen should pop up, then click on ARM

Feedback from mics

When speaking into the mic, the numbered knobs should turn green

If they are not turning green, make sure the mic is turned on and correctly connected to the MixPre10

Green means that the audio level is good

Yellow means that it's a bit too loud

Red means that it is very loud

Although you can potentially fix how much sound is coming through the microhpone, it's always best to ensure that feedback stays green

Adjusting headphone grain

If the sound coming into your headpones is too loud and your microphone is already set around 48 to 52 then you can adjust the headphone grain

move the knob numbered 21 on the image until the screen tells you the headphone grain level is 72 or wherever you think is not too loud

I chose the number 72 because it seems to be not too loud and not too low, and have been using it for every podcast that was recorded.

Setting up projects

To set up a new project, you have to click on the hamburger menu which is numbered 5

Then click on Project

Then be sure to click on New and then click Date since it's easier to know by date than by name

After setting up the project, you can change the name of the file by going back to home

Then click on the title of the project on the MixPre10 screen, it is numbered 2

You can click on edit, and then choose a new name for your project

To choose a letter you have to use the headphone knob. To select a letter, push in on the same knob

Recording a project

To begin recording, move the red knob up (it is numbered 4)

You should hear a beep once you start, and the light should come out of the red knob

To stop recording, just push the red knob, and you will hear a boop along with the light turning off around the knob

To listen to what you recorded on the MixPre10, you can push the knob down to play the recording.

While listening to a recording, you can push the red knob left or right to fast forward or rewind

To stop listening, just push on the red knob again.

To listen to other recordings, move the red knob left or right.

Exporting your project

Entering transfer mode

Once you are finished recording, click on the hamburger icon to go to the menu

Then continue clicking on the Menu text until you see a System option

Click on System

Finally you can click on File transfer

The screen should then say it's in file transfer mode

Transferring to your computer

You need USB-C cable to be able to export your project onto your computer

Connect the USB-C cable to the port numbered 14

Then connect it to your computer

The MixPre10 should show up like if it was a USB or a drive

Navigate through your filesystem, find the date of the project

Then make sure you Copy the file to wherever you please. It's always best to leave a backup on the MixPre10 in case something goes wrong.

Once you are finished copying, eject the MixPre10 and then exit the FileTransfer mode
